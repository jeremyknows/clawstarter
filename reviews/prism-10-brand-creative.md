## 1. Brand Voice & Personality

### Current State Analysis

**What the site currently communicates:**
- **Visual:** Premium, polished, accessible (Glacial Depths works well)
- **Copy:** Clean but generic ("Your Personal AI, Running on Your Mac")
- **Tone:** Professional, safe, a bit corporate
- **Personality:** Invisible (could be any SaaS product)

**The gap:** The site *looks* premium but *sounds* generic. The visual identity is 80% there; the voice is 30% there.

---

### Brand Personality Framework: "The Seasoned Guide"

ClawStarter isn't a product; it's **a guide who's been there, learned the hard way, and wants to save you the trouble.**

**Core Attributes:**

1. **Honest**
   - Never oversells ("15 minutes" not "2 minutes")
   - Acknowledges what won't work ("Not for true beginners")
   - Admits when competitors are better ("Most people should use ChatGPT")
   - **Why it matters:** Trust is the rarest commodity in tech marketing

2. **Opinionated**
   - Has a point of view (battle-tested > theoretical)
   - Makes recommendations, not just options ("Use OpenRouter" not "Here are 6 providers")
   - Simplifies complexity through curation
   - **Why it matters:** Analysis paralysis kills conversions; guidance builds confidence

3. **Generous**
   - Gives away the playbook (templates, scripts, learnings)
   - Prioritizes user success over vendor lock-in
   - Celebrates when users outgrow it
   - **Why it matters:** Generosity creates advocates

4. **Technical Without Gatekeeping**
   - Uses precise language ("persistent memory" not "smart AI")
   - Explains without condescending ("Terminal is like following a recipe")
   - Respects user intelligence
   - **Why it matters:** Technical-curious users hate being talked down to

5. **Calm Confidence**
   - No hype, no urgency manipulation, no FOMO
   - Trusts the product to speak for itself
   - "This works. Here's proof. Try it or don't."
   - **Why it matters:** Exhausted by breathless startup marketing

**Personality Archetype:** The Field Guide (Sage + Explorer)
- **Not:** The Salesperson (Hero), The Entertainer (Jester), The Authority (Ruler)
- **Similar to:** Basecamp, Notion (early days), The Wirecutter

---

### Voice Spectrum Positioning

| Dimension | Position | Example |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Formality** | Casual-professional (60% casual) | "Here's how to..." not "One must configure..." |
